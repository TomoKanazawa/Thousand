## Abstract
This multicenter study examined whether a clinical decision support system (CDSS) improves outcomes for hospitalized patients with acute kidney injury. Researchers analyzed 528,108 patients across 12 months before and 24 months after CDSS implementation. Among the 64,512 patients diagnosed with AKI, mortality decreased from 10.2% to 9.4% (odds ratio 0.91; 95% CI 0.86-0.96; P=0.001). Hospital length of stay fell from 9.3 to 9.0 days. In adjusted models, mortality odds ratio was 0.76 (95% CI 0.70-0.83) and dialysis use was 0.66 (95% CI 0.61-0.72). The findings demonstrated that implementation of a CDSS for AKI resulted in a small but sustained decrease in hospital mortality, dialysis use, and length of stay.

## Key numbers
- N = 528,108 patients; 64,512 with AKI
- Mortality 10.2% → 9.4% (OR 0.91, p=0.001)
- LOS 9.3 → 9.0 days
