## Abstract
Abstract unavailable verbatim without paywall access. Per Health Affairs landing page and secondary reporting: the study used 2019 MA encounter data to evaluate the impact of Health Risk Assessments (HRAs) on Hierarchical Condition Categories (HCC) risk scores, variation across contracts, and aggregate payment impact. Findings: 44.4% of MA beneficiaries had at least one HRA; among those, HCC scores increased on average 12.8% as a result of HRAs; restricting the risk-score impact of HRAs could reduce spending up to $12.3 billion per year.

## Key numbers
- 44.4% of MA beneficiaries had ≥1 HRA
- +12.8% mean HCC score uplift from HRAs (among those with one)
- Up to $12.3B/yr aggregate MA payment attributable to HRA-driven coding
