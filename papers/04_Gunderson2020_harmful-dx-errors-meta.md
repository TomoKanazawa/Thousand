## Abstract
Diagnostic error represents "a missed, delayed or wrong diagnosis" and constitutes a significant patient safety concern. This meta-analysis pooled data from 22 studies encompassing 80,026 patients with 760 documented harmful diagnostic errors. The pooled rate was 0.7% (95% CI 0.5% to 1.1%) of adult hospital admissions. Among detailed cases, malignancy and pulmonary embolism were the most frequently missed diagnoses. The authors estimate this translates to approximately 249,900 harmful diagnostic errors annually in the USA. They conclude that a wide range of common diagnoses are missed and that improvement efforts should target fundamental diagnostic processes, including both cognitive and systemic factors.

## Key numbers
- 0.7% pooled harmful dx error rate (95% CI 0.5–1.1%) across 80,026 patients
- ~249,900 harmful dx errors/year in US hospitals
- Malignancy and PE most frequently missed
