## Abstract
Although awareness of chronic kidney disease (CKD) is low in outpatient settings, the prevalence of unrecognized CKD in the inpatient setting is unknown. The researchers examined unlabeled CKD among adults admitted to a North Carolina tertiary-care hospital using medical records, ICD-9 codes, and laboratory data from 2000-2005. Among 9,772 patients with sufficient data, 431 had stage 5 CKD and 6,851 had stage 2-4 CKD. Notably, the number of patients not labeled as having CKD by ICD-9 code was 2,176 (72.5%). The study found that a large proportion of individuals with CKD are going unrecognized in the hospital setting and suggested that automatic laboratory GFR reporting and CKD education could improve awareness.

## Key numbers
- 72.5% of inpatients with lab-defined CKD were not coded with CKD
- Population: 9,772 inpatients, 2000–2005, tertiary-care center
