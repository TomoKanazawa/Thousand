## Abstract
Abstract unavailable verbatim without paywall access. Per search-result summaries, the study describes frequency, demographics, and outcomes of patients with delayed PE diagnosis; in a related 2020 Respiration cohort (Mansella et al.), delayed diagnosis was established in 44 of 226 cases (19.5%). The Walen body of work documents that diagnostic delay of PE is substantial, with much of it accruing in patient-delay and primary-care delay phases; presenting with chest pain or DVT symptoms was associated with earlier diagnosis.

## Key numbers (from related cohort)
- Delayed diagnosis in ~20% of PE cases in comparable cohorts

## Why this matters for Andy
PE remains a top-missed inpatient dx (Gunderson #04). Quantifying delay frequency frames the addressable signal for an Andy PE-trigger module.
