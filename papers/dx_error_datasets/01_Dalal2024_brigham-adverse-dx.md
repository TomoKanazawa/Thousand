# 01 — Dalal 2024 — Adverse diagnostic events in hospitalised patients

> **Stub (no PDF — paywalled, BMJ Qual Saf).** Compiled from the abstract +
> PubMed. Verify methods against full text before quoting in detail.

- **Citation:** Dalal AK, Plombon S, Konieczny K, et al; Patient Safety Learning Laboratory Adjudicator Group. *Adverse diagnostic events in hospitalised patients: a single-centre, retrospective cohort study.* BMJ Qual Saf. 2025;34(6):377–388. doi:10.1136/bmjqs-2024-017183. PMID 39353737.
- **Access:** single-centre PHI → collaboration/IRB, not download.

## Design / N
- Single centre (**Brigham / Mass General Brigham**), general-medicine inpatients.
- **9,147 eligible → 675 reviewed** (stratified: 100% of ICU transfers, 38.5% of 90-day deaths, 7% complex, 2.4% lower-risk), weighted to population estimates.
- **Labels:** 2 trained adjudicators per case using the **Safer Dx instrument** (likelihood of diagnostic error) + **DEER taxonomy** (process failures); expert panels resolved uncertain cases. **Full-record** retrospective review.

## Key numbers
- Harmful diagnostic errors **7.2%** (95% CI 4.66–9.80); preventable 6.1%; **severely harmful 1.1%**.
- **Delays characterised 61.9% of harmful errors** → predominantly *revealed/delayed*, not silent-undetected.
- ~1 in 14 general-medicine inpatients affected.

## Relevance as a data source
- **Gold-standard physician-adjudicated labels** (Safer Dx) on real inpatient records — the validation labels our KDIGO lab proxy lacks.
- Caveat: trigger-based + delay-dominated → captures *surfaced* errors, not the silent never-detected segment we target. Best for adjudication method + label-paired validation.
