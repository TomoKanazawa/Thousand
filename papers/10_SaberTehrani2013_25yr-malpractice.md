## Abstract
This research analyzed 350,706 paid malpractice claims from the National Practitioner Data Bank (1986-2010). Key findings included: "Diagnostic errors (n=100,249) were the leading type (28.6%) and accounted for the highest proportion of total payments (35.2%)." Diagnostic errors resulted in death more frequently than other claim types: "Diagnostic errors more often resulted in death than other allegation groups (40.9% vs 23.9%, p<0.001)." The study found that "the inflation-adjusted, 25-year sum of diagnosis-related payments was US$38.8 billion (mean per-claim payout US$386,849; median US$213,250; IQR US$74,545–484,500)." The authors concluded: "Among malpractice claims, diagnostic errors appear to be the most common, most costly and most dangerous of medical mistakes."

## Key numbers
- 28.6% of paid malpractice claims = diagnostic errors (leading category)
- 35.2% of total payments
- 40.9% of dx-error claims resulted in death (vs 23.9% other)
- $38.8B inflation-adjusted payouts over 25 years
