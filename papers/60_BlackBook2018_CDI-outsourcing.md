# 60 — Black Book 2018: 65% of >200-bed hospitals outsource CDI

**Status:** Industry market-research survey; press-release-grade summary.

## Source
Black Book Market Research. New Generation CDI Enhances Patient Care and Reduces Financial Risk (Oct 2018). Survey of 2,920 health leaders.

## Confidence
Market-research-vendor survey; widely cited in trade press. Self-reported and not peer-reviewed.

## URL
- https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/black-book-survey-new-generation-cdi-enhances-patient-care-and-reduces-financial-risk-300734394.html

## Key numbers (per Black Book press release)
- 65% of hospitals >200 beds outsource CDI audit/review/programming in 2018 (up from 24% in 2015)
- 91% of >150-bed hospitals outsourcing CDI in Q3 reported >$2.1M minimum revenue/reimbursement uplift
- Sample: 2,920 health leaders

## Why this matters for Andy
Validates that hospital CDI is already a heavily-outsourced workflow — i.e., a buyer category accustomed to bringing in third-party software/services. Lowers the GTM friction for an Andy CDI-augmentation pitch.
