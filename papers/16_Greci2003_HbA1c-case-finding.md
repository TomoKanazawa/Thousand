## Abstract
The study evaluated HbA1c measurement's role in identifying diabetes among hospitalized patients presenting with elevated blood glucose. Between March and July 2000, 508 emergency department admissions were screened for hyperglycemia (plasma glucose >125 mg/dl). Of 50 eligible consenting patients without preexisting diabetes or corticosteroid use, 35 completed the study with 21 receiving a diabetes diagnosis.

Diabetic patients showed significantly higher HbA1c levels compared to non-diabetic subjects (6.8±0.4% versus 5.3±0.1%, P=0.002). An HbA1c threshold of >6.0% demonstrated 100% specific (14/14) and 57% sensitive (12/21) for the diagnosis of diabetes. Conversely, a cutoff of 5.2% yielded opposite characteristics. The authors concluded that HbA1c measurement >6.0% reliably confirms diabetes and <5.2% effectively excludes it in acutely ill hospitalized patients with hyperglycemia.

## Key numbers
- 21/35 hyperglycemic inpatients without prior dx diagnosed with diabetes (60%)
- HbA1c >6.0% = 100% specific, 57% sensitive
- HbA1c <5.2% effectively excludes diabetes
