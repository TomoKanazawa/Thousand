## MVP Dataset Selection for Differential Diagnosis Copilot

### Decision framework

The new product diagnoses. To build and validate a DDx copilot, every dataset must answer one of three questions:

1. **What does a real diagnostic encounter look like?** (training data: presentation → diagnosis)
2. **What's the ground-truth differential for these symptoms?** (labeled DDx data)
3. **How accurately does our system reason diagnostically?** (evaluation benchmarks)

Datasets are selected against these three criteria, not against generic "is it good clinical data" reasoning.

---

### Primary datasets (core MVP build)

#### 1. MIMIC-IV + MIMIC-IV-Note — promoted to primary

**What it is:** 65K+ ICU patients from Beth Israel Deaconess. Includes 331K discharge summaries and 2.3M radiology reports written by real physicians. Already converted to FHIR R4 format (5.84M resources).

**Why it's now primary (was style reference):** A discharge summary is the perfect DDx training artifact — it contains the chief complaint, HPI, exam findings, labs, imaging, the differential considered, and the final diagnosis. Each note is a complete labeled case: presentation → reasoning → ground-truth answer.

**Why ICU bias is now an asset:** Under the old idea (PCP pre-visit prep), ICU data was a mismatch. For DDx, the highest-stakes diagnostic moments happen in ICU and ED — exactly where missed/wrong diagnoses cause the 795K Americans/year of harm cited in the new pitch. ICU presentations are also more diagnostically rich (more labs, more imaging, more documented reasoning) than outpatient encounters.

**Access:** PhysioNet credentialing (CITI training + DUA, days to weeks). Free.

#### 2. DDXPlus — purpose-built for DDx

**What it is:** 1.3M synthetic patients across 49 medical conditions, each with structured symptoms, antecedents, and a labeled differential diagnosis. Generated by a probabilistic medical knowledge base validated by physicians. Released alongside a NeurIPS 2022 paper.

**Why it matters:** This is the one dataset designed specifically for training and evaluating differential diagnosis systems. Symptoms map to ranked DDx with explicit probabilities — you can compute precision@k, recall@k, and ranking metrics directly. No other resource gives you this scale of labeled DDx pairs.

**Why both DDXPlus and MIMIC:** MIMIC gives you real messy presentations but biased to ICU. DDXPlus gives you labeled DDx coverage across 49 conditions including primary care complaints. Together they cover both "looks like reality" and "is labeled for DDx training."

**Access:** Open (Figshare/GitHub). No credentialing.

#### 3. MIMIC-IV-Ext-CDM (Clinical Decision Making)

**What it is:** A MIMIC-IV extension specifically reformatted for evaluating LLM diagnostic reasoning on abdominal pathologies. Cases are structured as iterative diagnostic encounters where the model orders tests, interprets results, and proposes diagnoses.

**Why it matters:** This is the bridge between MIMIC's real cases and DDXPlus's labeled DDx structure. It evaluates the actual diagnostic reasoning loop our copilot performs, not just static accuracy on pre-formatted questions.

**Access:** PhysioNet (same credentialing as MIMIC-IV).

---

### Secondary datasets (hard-case benchmarks)

#### 4. NEJM Case Records (Case Records of the Massachusetts General Hospital)

**What it is:** Weekly clinical case challenges published in NEJM since 1923. Each is a complex diagnostic puzzle with the final diagnosis revealed at the end. Approximately 50 new cases per year.

**Why it matters:** Every published medical AI paper benchmarks against NEJM Case Records because they're the recognized hard-mode test of diagnostic reasoning. Beating GPT-5.2 / Claude Opus baselines on NEJM cases is the strongest single proof point for KOL credibility.

**Access:** Publicly readable (paywall for full archive but text accessible via institutional access; existing curated subsets exist in research papers).

#### 5. Synthea — demoted to supporting

**What it is:** Open-source patient generator producing FHIR R4 bundles with longitudinal medical histories.

**Why it's still useful:** Need volume of plausible FHIR-format patients to test the ingestion pipeline, the SMART on FHIR launch flow, and Epic API parsing without burning MIMIC quota. Also useful for stress-testing the system against patient diversity that MIMIC doesn't cover (pediatric, OB, healthy populations).

**Why it's no longer core:** Synthea has no clinical notes (its biggest gap was solved by MIMIC having real notes). The Chatty-Notes pipeline and error injection layer from the old plan are no longer needed — MIMIC provides authentic messiness for free.

**Access:** Open-source. Apache 2.0.

---

### Evaluation benchmarks (measure accuracy)

| Dataset | What it tests | Why we need it |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **MedQA (USMLE)** | Multiple-choice clinical vignettes from US medical licensing exam | The standard public benchmark — every frontier model reports MedQA scores. Required for comparing against published baselines. |
| **MedMCQA** | 194K medical entrance exam questions across 21 specialties | Specialty breadth coverage that MedQA lacks. |
| **PubMedQA** | Biomedical Q&A grounded in PubMed abstracts | Tests evidence-grounded reasoning — important since the copilot must cite sources. |
| **NEJM Healer** | Structured clinical reasoning cases from NEJM | Closer to real clinical reasoning workflow than multiple choice. |

---

### What we dropped from the old plan and why

| Old core dataset | Status | Why dropped |
|---|---|---|
| **Synthea as foundation** | Demoted | DDx needs real diagnostic narrative, which Synthea can't generate |
| **Chatty-Notes pipeline** | Removed | Built to compensate for Synthea's missing notes — MIMIC has 331K real ones |
| **Error injection layer** | Removed | The old product detected EHR messiness; the new product diagnoses regardless of messiness. Adding noise hurts MVP, not helps. |
| **i2b2 Temporal Relations** | Removed | Specific to temporal supersession problem. DDx doesn't care which note overrides which. |
| **MedNLI** | Removed | Tests contradiction detection between sentences. Not relevant to DDx ranking. |
| **EMNLP 2023 Clinical Contradiction** | Removed | Same reason as MedNLI. |

---

### What we kept and re-prioritized

| Dataset | Old role | New role |
|---|---|---|
| **MIMIC-IV-Note** | Style reference | **Primary training/eval data** |
| **Synthea** | Core foundation | Supporting (FHIR pipeline only) |
| **PhysioNet credentialing** | Optional path | **Critical path** — blocks MIMIC and CDM access |

---

### Build-order recommendation

1. **Week 1:** Start PhysioNet credentialing (CITI training + DUA). It blocks the most important datasets and takes the longest.
2. **Week 1 in parallel:** Pull DDXPlus (open access). Build the DDx ranking interface against labeled symptom → diagnosis pairs.
3. **Week 2:** Pull Synthea data. Wire up FHIR ingestion pipeline against synthetic FHIR bundles.
4. **Week 2-3:** Once PhysioNet approved, ingest MIMIC-IV on FHIR + MIMIC-IV-Note. This becomes the training/eval substrate.
5. **Week 3-4:** Pull MIMIC-IV-Ext-CDM for diagnostic reasoning loop evaluation.
6. **Week 4+:** Add MedQA/MedMCQA/PubMedQA for public benchmark comparisons. Curate NEJM Case Records subset for hard-case validation.

### Bottom line

**MIMIC-IV + DDXPlus is the MVP foundation.** MIMIC supplies real labeled diagnostic encounters; DDXPlus supplies scaled labeled DDx pairs. NEJM Case Records gives us the hard-mode benchmark. MedQA/MedMCQA/PubMedQA give us the public scoreboard. Synthea remains as the FHIR pipeline test bed. Everything else from the old plan goes.
